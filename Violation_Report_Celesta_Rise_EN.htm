II. Overview of the Process and Violations

The violations are concentrated primarily in A13/GA concerning the GA area attached to the Garden Unit.

The GA is a lengthy appendix (9 pages) containing numerous clauses governing the rights and obligations of the parties, the method of measuring the GA area, the right to use the garden (not ownership rights), and the handover minutes for the GA area. Accordingly, the GA falls within the category of documents that must be registered under the Law on Consumer Rights Protection, specifically Article 28 on general transaction conditions.

However, the Developer failed to register the GA with the VCC. In the course of our dealings, I identified numerous violations in the GA and compelled the Developer to make revisions. These revisions represented very significant changes compared to the originally issued version, resulting in materially different content and legal consequences, fundamentally altering the rights and obligations of the Buyer compared to the GA signed by other Garden Unit owners previously.

The Developerï¿½s failure to register the GA, combined with issuing revised versions with differing content to different buyers, gives rise to serious legal risks and directly infringes upon consumer rights.

In particular, one critical violation is that the GA area ownership is not clearly identified in the SPA, in serious breach of Article 142, Clause 3 of the 2023 Housing Law, which requires the SPA to clearly identify common areas and private areas.

This violation not only affects the SPA of Garden Unit owners but may also affect all other SPAs in the project. In practice, the Developer ignored my legitimate questions for an extended period, delayed responses for many months, and then continued to treat the matter as an isolated case in order to unilaterally adjust it according to its own will.
